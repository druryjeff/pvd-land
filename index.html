Sitting stylishly between Boston and New York, amidst a wealth of historic architecture, is a thriving local culture with art, design, food, shops, entertainment and a diverse population of really nice people. Get in there. Explore the neighborhoods.

An easy way into the heart of PVD is through the neighborhoods. These four hoods are our picks for a cool-filled morning, noon or night.

  • East Side
  • West Side
  • South Side
  • Down City

East Side

The East Side is home to Brown University and the Rhode Island School of Design. Historic architecture and unique shops are some of the ways to spend your time here. A short walk up the hill from Down City.

Featured Destination

The RISD Museum is unlike most museums. The museum is housed across a series of buildings that span from South Main Street to Benefit Street. From modern to antiquity, the collection has something for most people. (Above: The main gallery at the RISD Museum)

Downcity

The Down City area is small but densely populated with some of the finest restaurants in the region. In addition, some of the best shopping – from boutiques, to book stores, to skateboards – can be found within walking distance.
